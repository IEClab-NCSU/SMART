Legume plants have bacteria in their roots that obtain nitrogen gas from the air and convert it to a form that the plants can use as a nutrient.  Many plants also are host to fungi that live in their roots and help supply nutrients to the plants.  In both cases, the plants grow faster when the microbes are present and the microbes depend on the plants for food and a place to live.
