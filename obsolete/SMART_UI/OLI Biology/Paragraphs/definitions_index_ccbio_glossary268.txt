A substance having no medicinal benefit that is administered to the
                                control group in a study so that these study participants do no know
                                if they are in the control or experimental group. Placebos in drug
                                trials are often sugar pills or a vitamin formulated to look like
                                the drug being tested. Interestingly, sometimes patients taking a
                                placebo will report that they notice an improvement in the medical
                                condition the drug is designed to treat or even experience a real
                                medical improvement all because they think they are taking the new
                                drug. This phenomenon is called the placebo effect.
